Stated Factors of Traveler Use of Transit Before, During and After the COVID-19 Pandemic

The COVID-19 pandemic caused a significant decrease in transit ridership. Whereas highway travel rebounded quickly to the pandemic impacts, a concern exists that transit users might not fully return in the short or medium term. For this paper, a travel survey was designed with Perceived Stress Scores and Fear of Covid Instruments, alongside demographic questions. I developed a Random Forest to predict if the user decreased or increased its user transit. In addition, I interpreted the RF decisions with Additive Feature Attributions techniques as SHAP Values to remark which were the most impactful features on the model as why an user increased or decreased their public transit.

Artificial Neural Networks for Part-of-Speech Tagger

Current Part-of-Speech taggers accuracy lies in a asymptote difficult to cross, as they face problems handling ambiguity and information they have not seen. Therefore, the approach suggested in this project considers linguistic principles in the design of the stochastic model. The methods to follow consists of the experimentation with a Deep Feedforward and Bididirectional LSTM networks, comparing how Long-Short Term Memory layers aids the system with a track of time, each word acting as a state. After the training concluded, the network reflected a training accuracy of 99% and a validation accuracy of 97%. The results obtained validates our approach and allows it to be built upon for further experimentation.

Analysis in the Study of Emotion and Social Media Consumption in Puerto Rico

Advances in technology and the tendency of using social media to express opinions and emotions in real time has provided an opportunity to study how different events influence these opinions. Additionally, literature suggests that social media consumption increments in emergency events. This report presents preliminary results in the study of social media consumption and its relationship with how positive or negatives were the reaction of Twitter users in Puerto Rico from November 2019 to March 2021, where it is known that the country confronted earthquakes and the Covid19 pandemic. Also, this project makes use of unsupervised learning techniques to cluster users based on topic categories. Preliminary results show that there was an increment in social media consumption and that users tend to cluster in two different groups.

COVID-19: Word Clustering

In response to the COVID-19 pandemic, the White House and a coalition of leading research groups have prepared the COVID-19 Open Research Dataset (CORD-19). CORD-19 is a resource of over 1,000,000 scholarly articles, including over 400,000 with full text, about COVID-19, SARS-CoV-2, and related coronaviruses. When the pandemic quarantine started, I volunteered in a Kaggle challenge to explore the CORD-19 data-set with more than a million scientific articles. I implemented a Word Clustering to explore the articles and get the similar word based by the inputted terms. The techniques used for the word Clustering were FastText and t-Distributed Stochastic Neighbor Embedding.
